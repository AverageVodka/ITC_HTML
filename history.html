About Us

Philippine cuisine is a rich and flavorful tapestry woven from centuries of history and cultural influences. Long before colonization, early Filipinos used native cooking techniques like grilling, steaming, and fermentation, relying on ingredients such as rice, coconut, fish, and tropical fruits. The arrival of Chinese traders introduced dishes like pancit and lumpia, while the Spanish colonial period brought profound changes, including the adoption of adobo, lechon, and decadent desserts like bibingka and ensaymada. Fiestas and religious celebrations also became occasions for culinary creativity. During the American era, the introduction of canned goods, baked items like pan de sal, and fast food further shaped the Filipino palate. Japanese influence left its mark with techniques like pickling and dishes such as tempura. In modern times, globalization has brought new flavors and techniques while Filipino cuisine continues to celebrate its roots in communal feasts, family gatherings, and regional specialties. Each dish tells a story of resilience, creativity, and a shared love for food, making Philippine cuisine a unique blend of tradition and innovation that continues to evolve today.
