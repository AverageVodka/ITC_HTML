Lumpiang Ubod

Lumpiang Ubod is a fresh spring roll from Negros Island, filled with tender coconut palm heart (ubod), mixed vegetables, and sometimes shrimp or pork. Wrapped in a soft, delicate crepe, it’s served with a sweet-savory peanut sauce and garnished with crushed peanuts and garlic. A signature dish of Negros, it reflects the island’s love for fresh, local ingredients and subtle flavors.

  • 1 lb. hearts of palm ubod
  • 1 piece Knorr Shrimp Cube
  • 4 ounces pork thinly sliced
  • 12 pieces shrimp deveined
  • 1 carrot julienne
  • 2 tablespoons parsley chopped
  • 1 piece onion chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic crushed
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste
  • 8 pieces lettuce leaves
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanut crushed
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 pieces eggs
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or cooking oil spray
  • 6 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 6 cloves garlic crushed
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  1. Make the lumpia filling. Start by heating 3 tablespoons oil in a pan. Saute onion and garlic until the onion softens. 1 piece onion, 3 tablespoons cooking oil, 4 cloves garlic
  2. Add pork and saute until light brown. Pour 1 ½ cup water into the pan. Let boil. 4 ounces pork, 1 cup water
  3. Add Knorr Shrimp Cube. Stir. Continue boiling in medium heat until the liquid evaporates. 1 piece Knorr Shrimp Cube
  4. Add hearts of palm (ubod). Cook for 5 minutes. Toss every few seconds. 1 lb. hearts of palm.
  5. Add carrots. Cook for 3 minutes. 1 carrot .
  6. Put the shrimp into the pan. Toss and continue cooking until shrimp turns orange. 12 pieces shrimp
  7. Add chopped parsley and season with ground black pepper and fish sauce. Set aside. 2 tablespoons parsley, Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste
  8. Make the sauce by pouring water in a saucepan. Let it boil. 1 1/2 cups water
  9. Add brown sugar and soy sauce. Stir until completely diluted. 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce, 6 tablespoons brown sugar
  10. Put the garlic in the saucepan. Cook for 20 seconds. 6 cloves garlic
  11. Combine cornstarch with 3 tablespoons water. Stir until blended. Pour the mixture into the pan. Continuously stir while cooking until mixture thickens. Set aside. 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  12. Make the fresh lumpia wrapper by beating eggs in a bowl. 2 pieces eggs
  13. Stir-in cooking oil and add water. Gradually add all-purpose flour. Continue stirring until all ingredients are well blended. Note: you can let the mixture pass through a strainer to filter lumps. 1 cup all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons canola oil, 1 1/2 cups water
  14. Cook the wrapper by heating a non-stick pan. Grease it with oil. Pour wrapper mixture into the pan enough to cover the area. Continue cooking using medium heat until the wrapper can freely slide on the pan when you shake it. Transfer the wrapper to a clean plate. Note: do the same step until the mixture is fully consumed. 1/4 cup cooking oil or cooking oil spray
  15. Assemble the lumpiang ubod by laying a piece of wrapper flat on a plate. Place a piece of lettuce leaf over the wrapper. Scoop a good amount of filling (hearts of palm) over the lettuce. Gently wrap. 8 pieces lettuce leaves
  16. Arrange the wrapped lumpia on a serving plate. Pour sauce over it and finish by topping it with crushed peanuts. 1/2 cup roasted peanut
  17. Serve. Share and enjoy!

Chicken Inasal

Chicken Inasal is a beloved grilled chicken dish from Panay Island, particularly Iloilo. Marinated in a mix of calamansi, garlic, ginger, vinegar, and annatto oil, it’s grilled to perfection, giving it a distinct smoky, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor. Often served with garlic rice, a dipping sauce of soy, calamansi, and chili, and a side of atchara (pickled papaya), Chicken Inasal is a hallmark of Western Visayan cuisine.

  • 2 lbs chicken cut into serving pieces
  • 2 tablespoons ginger minced
  • 2 tablespoons garlic minced
  • 3/4 cup lemongrass chopped
  • 1 cup coconut vinegar
  • 1/2 cup lemon or calamansi juice
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup lemon soda softdrink
  • 1/2 tablespoon ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons annatto oil atsuete oil
  • 1/2 cup margarine softened
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon lemon or calamansi juice
  1. In a freezer bag or large bowl, combine chicken, lemongrass, salt, ground black pepper, ginger, garlic, brown sugar, vinegar, lemon-lime soda, and lemon juice.
  2. Stir or shake the mixture until every ingredient is well incorporated. Marinade for 1 to 3 hours.
  3. In a bowl, combine margarine, annatto oil, salt, and lemon juice then stir. Set aside.
  4. Grill the chicken while basting generous amount of the margarine mixture.
  5. Transfer the grilled chicken to a serving plate.
  6. Serve with sinamak. Share and enjoy!

Pucherong Bisaya

Pucherong Bisaya is Cebu’s take on the classic Spanish-inspired pochero, a comforting stew made with tender pork or beef, saba bananas, sweet potatoes, and vegetables like cabbage and string beans. Simmered in a flavorful broth, it’s slightly sweeter than other versions due to the bananas and is often paired with rice and a side of fish sauce (patis) for added flavor. This hearty dish reflects Cebuano ingenuity in blending local ingredients with colonial influences.

  • 1.5 kg beef shanks, cut into sections
  • 4 pcs saba bananas, sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots
  • 2 ears fresh sweet corn, cut into 3 sections
  • 2 bunches bok choy
  • 1 cabbage, quartered
  • 4 stalks lemongrass, white parts pounded
  • 6 stalks spring onions, sliced
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 piece ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • fish sauce
  • salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • Oil
  1. In a pot boil beef shanks for 15 minutes. Drain, rinse and remove any scum.
  2. In a clean pot, add oil then sauté garlic, ginger and onions.
  3. Add the beef shanks, corn, bamboo shoots, lemongrass and salt. Pour enough water to cover the meat then bring it to a boil. Simmer for 1 1/2 hours or until beef is tender.
  4. Add the saba bananas, continue to cook for 15 minutes.
  5. Add the cabbage, continue to cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Add the bok choy and spring onions, continue to cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Season with fish sauce and freshly ground black pepper then serve.
